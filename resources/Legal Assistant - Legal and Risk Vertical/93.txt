SUMMARY
This position is responsible for assisting the Conflicts Supervisor, other members of the New Business Conflicts & Compliance Department (US LLP), and firm administration with corporate and conflicts database research. The position will assist with the review of conflicts of interest issues related to potential new business, new lateral candidates, business services employees, and various special interest initiatives in the firm, such as identification of sanctioned entities to ensure compliance with laws in certain jurisdictions and assisting with various special interest initiatives for the firm.
JOB DESCRIPTION
· Assist in the identification of potential conflicts of interest through corporate and conflicts database research of client and matter information provided by requesting attorneys, potential attorneys, assistants, and business services employees;
· Become proficient in understanding and utilization of conflicts of interest database;
· Assist with maintenance and update of the conflicts database;
· Research corporate history of clients and parties related to the former and new client work;
· Respond to lawyers’ and business services employees’ requests and perform conflicts of interest searches;
· Assist members of the New Business Conflicts and Compliance Department with a wide range of research requests and other projects to include general administrative duties including proofreading and filing; and
· Assist with a wide range of research requests and other projects related to the research of conflicts of interest issues as assigned; and
· All members of the firm are encouraged to participate in our Global Citizenship program.
QUALIFICATIONS
REQUIRED SKILLS
Elite database or comparable database experience a plus. Strict attention to detail is a must. Must also possess strong organizational and communication skills. Must be responsible for completing tasks and following through with the resolution of issues, including the ability to meet deadlines and work in a fast-paced environment. Must have data entry skills and knowledge of Microsoft Word.
EDUCATION, CERTIFICATIONS AND/OR EXPERIENCE:
At least one (1) year of conflicts of interest or comparable research skills preferred. Bachelor’s degree and experience of working in an office environment preferred.
HOURS
Core hours are Monday through Friday, 9:00 a.m. – 5:30 p.m., including dinner hour. Must be flexible to work additional hours.
