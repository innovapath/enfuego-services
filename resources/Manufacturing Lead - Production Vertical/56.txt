Position Summary
The responsibility of the Project Engineer, is to assist in the development, design and management of equipment projects related to "capital equipment" & "media" manufacturing, lead the activities required to evaluate and implement changes to materials and processes used to manufacture media, evaluate and implement new products into the manufacturing environment, and provide technical support to manufacturing to resolve challenging manufacturing issues.
Primary Responsibilities
· Lead moderate size and or support large-scale projects from initial conception through completion.
· Evaluates systems and or processes to isolate root causes and implements corrective actions.
· Generate and maintain detailed budgets and timelines, which includes all pertinent information related to specific projects assigned.
· Designs and executes experiments to solve problems related to improving or developing new processes using creative and innovative concepts.
· Design process, converting and test equipment for the use in " media" and membrane manufacturing along with "capital equipment manufacturing equipment."
· Supervise contractors (plumbers, electricians, etc) and coordinate mechanical assemblies.
· Coordinate testing of materials with internal and external laboratories.
· Evaluate and analyze results identifying solutions relative to customer requirements, problems and opportunities.
· Characterize manufacturing processes and materials to identify and understand critical process drivers and establish appropriate controls for these process drivers.
· Develop and lead qualification efforts for changes in manufacturing processes, changes in raw materials, and introduction of new products into production.
Skills And Knowledge
· Technical management of projects and processes
· Knowledge in the converting and or process industry with a strong background in mechanical, electrical or chemical engineering.
· Knowledge in chemical/polymer processing equipment, and/or fluid handling
· Working knowledge of industrial safety standards and regulations (OSHA).
· Contribute to programs which will encompass a variety of engineering disciplines utilizing in house as well as outside resources
· Knowledge of manufacturing processes and their direct impact on the final product as well as the associated business influences
· Working knowledge of manufacturing business unit practices and principles in a regulatory environment.
· Lead cross-functional technical teams.
· Working knowledge of SPC, Design of Experiments (DOE), Measurement Systems Analysis, and statistical hypothesis testing.
· Working knowledge and experience with product and process validation methods and documentation (IQ/OQ/PQ, Gage R&R).
· Working knowledge and experience with Lean Six Sigma and/or structured problem solving approaches.
· Working knowledge and experience with risk assessment and risk mitigation techniques.
· Proficient user of Microsoft Office, Autocad, Minitab, or other commonly used technical software.
· Bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering, Chemical Engineering or related discipline
· 2+ years of applicable progressive experience in converting, membrane and / or media technology development, implementation, and operations support environment required. Bachelor’s degree in mechanical or chemical engineering.
