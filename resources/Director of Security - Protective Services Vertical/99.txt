I: Job Summary
The Sr. Director, Information Security and Risk will partner with all Functional, Divisional and Site leadership to lead the global Information security program to ensure information assets are adequately protected and will be responsible for establishing and maintaining a corporate-wide, global information risk management program and organization. The role will also provide leadership of the IT Computer Systems Validation (CSV) team including computer systems validation oversight and execution for all applications designated as GxP designated validated systems.
Responsibilities include identifying, evaluating, protecting against and reporting on information security and GxP compliance risks globally in a manner that meets regulatory requirements, and aligns with and supports the risk posture of the enterprise. This individual will proactively work with business units to implement techniques and solutions and preventive technologies that meet defined policies and standards for Information Security and Computer System Validation and will oversee a variety of IT –related risk management activities.
II: Responsibilites
Key Responsibilities
· Lead Architecture development and Vendor Selection for security systems.
· Create, update and maintain policies and procedures that govern Systems Management and Configuration.
· Evolve IT Risk Management program inclusive of education, procedures, risk register maintenance, action plans and management reporting to ensure projects and activities are being performed in accordance with company’s risk appetite.
· Institute a robust security awareness program.
· Maintain and evolve the Disaster Recovery Program to ensure systems are appropriately protected and testing, training and exercises take place.
· Lead Incident Response: Assessing impact, identifying root cause, recommending actions and mobilizing resources in the event of a disruption.
· Provide leadership of the IT Computer Systems Validation (CSV) team including computer systems validation oversight and execution for all applications designated as GxP designated validated systems.
· Maintain and enhance the Vulnerability Management Program to provide appropriate protections for various system types including business tools, lab equipment and manufacturing systems.
· Oversee the FISMA compliance program adhering to NIST standards and multiple federal agency requirements to ensure continuation of Authorization to Operate status.
  Lead internal Information Security audit and assessment activities and coordinate remediation efforts.
· Member of the Information Technology leadership team, providing technical knowledge and managerial expertise, as well as assisting with the development of specific goal for the optimum use and management of IT resources.
