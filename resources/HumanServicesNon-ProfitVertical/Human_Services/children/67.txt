Save The Children UK

Job title: Humanitarian 
Job location: Washington, D.C., DC , US
Job description: 
The USP Protection Specialist will assist in the implementation of the US Programs Safety and Resilience Strategy. This person will be a key part of Save the Children’s response to emergencies in the United States, being responsible for the rapid assessment, development of program design and implementation of humanitarian protection programs (including psychosocial programming), and will oversee longer term recovery efforts after the 90 day emergency response phase has concluded.
This position reports to the Associate Director, Psychosocial and Protection Programs. They will be responsible for identifying and coordinating with relevant federal, state and non-governmental organizational partners to ensure smooth delivery of Save the Children emergency response and recovery programs. The Protection Specialist will be the lead for managing the day-to-day operations of response and recovery projects within the US and facilitating and implementing project evaluation and data collection.
This position performs various support duties and responds to inquiries and resolves matters of substantial complexity during emergency responses. Responsibilities require timely decision making and resourcefulness in challenging circumstances. Work efforts of this position impact client services, program implementation and the organization’s public image. As a front line representative of Save the Children, the position holder is required to ensure the safety and protection of children and families that he/she comes in contact with and represent the agency values of Accountability, Ambition, Collaboration, Creativity and Integrity.
Essential Duties, Responsibilities And Impact
· Lead child protection and safety programming in emergency response and recovery including: assessment, program development, training for and management of Child Friendly Spaces. Support humanitarian colleagues in implementation of emergency programs and establishment of recovery programming. Assist with building and maintaining relationships with schools, communities, partners and state contacts.Conduct site visits and monthly program fidelity checks during recovery programming. 45%
· Revise and update Child Protection in Emergency, CPiE, tools that are used during responses (e.g. The CPiE assessments tools, CPiE Referral Pathway tools, guidance for hosting parents meetings, etc) 10%
· Coordinate and support response and recovery staff in the integration of psychosocial programing into formal and informal educational activities. 20%
· Assist in identifying and building strategic relationships with partners to grow and expand protection program offerings. 5%
· Assist in the coordination of periodic reporting related to child safety and resilience programming. 5%
· Coordinate and conduct program trainings. 10%
· Work with the project team to assist in the development and implementation of the evaluation plan for emergency response and recovery programming. 5%
Required Background And Experience, Skills And Behaviors
Minimum of a High School Diploma with 3-5 years of experience. Bachelor’s degree in public health, early education, counseling, mental health, psychology or social work preferred. The ideal candidate will have at least 2 years of Project Management experience in an area related to this position. A valid driver’s license is also required. Must have strong oral and written communications skills and the capacity and willingness to be extremely flexible and accommodating in challenging working environments and knowledge of project management principles, practices, techniques and tools.
Must have strong organizational skills with experience in scheduling and event/training coordination. Must demonstrate ability to read and interpret evaluation-related documents, and offer recommendations based on findings. Must demonstrate ability to present information and respond to questions from groups or individuals. Must demonstrate ability to create and produce clear, concise paper and electronic documents. Must demonstrate knowledge and skill in using Microsoft Office Suite. Must demonstrate ability to work effectively with diverse levels of individuals and coordinate with many different agencies and organizations. Must demonstrate project management experience preferred with exceptional organizational and time management skills. Must show skill in organizing resources and establishing priorities. Must have ability to multi-task multiple projects at the same time. Excellent attention to detail, flexible and adaptable to change. Ability to apply common sense understanding to carry out instructions furnished in written, oral, or diagram form. Ability to solve practical problems and deal with a variety of concrete variables in standardized situations.

Save the Children is committed to conducting its programs and operations in a manner that is safe for the children it serves and helping protect the children with whom Save the Children is in contact. All Save the Children Representatives are explicitly prohibited from engaging in any activity that may result in any kind of Child Abuse. In addition, it is Save the Children’s policy to create and proactively maintain an environment that aims to prevent and deter any actions and omissions, whether deliberate or inadvertent, that place children at the risk of any kind of Child Abuse. All Save the Children Representatives are expected to conduct themselves in a manner consistent with this commitment and obligation. Any violations of this policy will be treated as a serious issue.
