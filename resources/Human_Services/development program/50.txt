Job Description
The Division Chief Operating Officer (DCOO) is a member of the Division leadership team and assists the Division Vice President(s) (DVPs) to provide overall management of their Divisions and the regions therein. The DCOO shares in the accountability for achieving Division performance targets for mission; volunteer services; and operations, including budget development/monitoring, development of employee and volunteer personnel, implementation of real estate transactions, facilities management, fleet management, and Information Systems. The DCOO delivers against solid performance goals established for Humanitarian Services and supports the DVP by being a change agent to move the Division and regions through cultural and business change. The DCOO may also be called upon to perform Regional Reviews, manage specific projects, either as pilot programs or for enterprise-wide implementations.
Responsibilities
· Consolidated Functions: Serves as the Division Liaison with national headquarters units, including
Finance, Human Resources, Real Estate Services, Fleet Management, and Information Systems. Ensures that the Division and regions follow corporate policies and procedures related to carrying out the field responsibilities related to these functions.
· Division Goals: Assists DVP to develop, implement, monitor and evaluate performance of achieving
Divisional goals in support of corporate goals, related to mission delivery, volunteer services, fund raising and operations.
· Leadership Development: Serves as coach, mentor and resource for regional and Division leadership.
Consults with regions on board development as a year-round process. Working with the DVP,
plans/implements Division meetings and training/development programs for regional leadership. Participates in Division/region succession planning activities and assist in determining development plans for ensuring career growth opportunities for future leadership positions. With colleague DCOOs, plans, facilitates and updates New Executive Leadership Orientations held at National Headquarters several times a year. With DVP and appropriate Division Human Resources Manager, provides input on plans for informal and formal personnel performance improvement as needed.
· Volunteer Services: Serves as the Division liaison between NHQ Volunteer Services and Regional
Volunteer Officers (RVOs) to ensure that regions are carrying out the Volunteer Growth Strategy. Conducts monthly calls with the RVOs to share best practices, identify/resolve issues and to provide consultation on Volunteer Services priorities. Provides coaching as needed for implementation of the various Volunteer Services projects/activities.
· Finance/Financial Development: Working with the DVP and Division Finance team, develops the annual
expense and capital budgets; participates in the monthly Operations Reviews. May participate with the DVP and Division Fundraising Vice President (DFVP) in regional Fundraising Reviews and fiscal year Financial Development planning.
· Change Management: Working with the DVP, plans for and supports change management activities for
regional teams to embrace cultural and emerging organizational priorities. Included may be the performance of regional reviews to assess how well regions are carrying out the mission, volunteer engagement/ retention, and providing operational support. Develops/presents Regional Review recommendations to the DVP and regional leadership and provides ongoing support to implement the recommendations.
· Project Management: May lead and /or participate in Division or enterprise-wide projects and pilot programs.
· Acting DVP: In the absence of the DVP, either short or longer term, may serve in this capacity and will
report directly to the President, Humanitarian Services during the time in this role.
· Interim Regional Executive Officer: In the event of a vacancy in an RCEO position, may serve for a short
time in this capacity. For longer term needs, the DVP will appoint an Interim RCEO; the DCOO assists with the onboarding/goal setting and continuing consultation with the Interim during their assignment.
Qualifications
· Education: Bachelor’s Degree in Business, Finance, Non Profit Management, or similar field/equivalent
experience. MBA or equivalent a plus.
· Experience: Minimum of 10 years work experience with at least 5 years in a non- profit organization or
service organization preferred. Previous American Red Cross experience at the chapter level desirable.
· Managerial Experience: Minimum of 7 years directly related to managing and supervising personnel.
· Skills/Abilities: Ability to plan, prioritize, organize work to maximize performance. Excellent organizational skills and ability to work with attention to detail. Strong project management skills. Decisive, innovative and proactive decision-making with strong vision and implementation skills as well as well-developed crisis management and time management skills. Strong communication skills (written and verbal). Demonstrated interpersonal skills, including negotiating, coaching consulting and influencing others. Refined critical thinking, analytical and problem-solving skills and demonstrated ability to excel in a collaborative team environment; ability to lead/influence teams to establish and adjust to current and emerging priorities.
· Other: Proficient with Microsoft Office Software including Word, Excel, Power Point and Outlook.
· Travel: Extensive travel is required throughout the Division and also to leadership meetings several times a year at National Headquarters or other locations throughout the United States.
Competencies
· Organizational agility, Deal with ambiguity, Manage complexity, Interpersonal savvy, Professionalism and integrity, Manage Vision and Purpose
Essential Functions/Physical Requirements
· Depending on work assignment, may require ordinary ambulatory skills sufficient to visit other locations; or the ability to stand, walk and manipulate (lift, carry, move) light to medium weights up to 20 pounds. Requires good hand-eye coordination, arm, hand and finger dexterity, including ability to grasp, and visual acuity to use a keyboard, operate equipment and read technical information. Overall mobility is essential including sitting for long periods of time, driving a vehicle and working under challenging conditions.
Work Conditions
· Work is performed indoors with some potential for exposure to safety and health hazards related to emergency services relief work. May travel and participate in meetings and conferences throughout region and/or chapter jurisdiction, state and Red Cross system. Because the Red Cross is a disaster response organization, staff may be asked to be on 24-hour call during period of major disaster.
