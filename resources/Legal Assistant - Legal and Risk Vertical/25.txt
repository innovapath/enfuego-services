GENERAL SUMMARY
Position provides administrative support to General Counsel and associates. Researches and analyzes law sources such as statutes, recorded judicial decisions, legal articles, and legal codes to prepare legal documents. The incumbent must exercise considerable initiative, drive, independent judgment, motivation, confidentiality, and discretion in handling issues, matters, projects and delegated assignments.
ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS
· Setting and managing files and work project lists
· Arranging meetings and managing calendars
· Answering and disposing of requests for information
· Assisting in document signature process
· Preparing initial drafts of standard contracts as well as non-legal documents and responses to government agencies
· Processing purchase orders and payment of invoices
· Answering calls and sorting through correspondence and mail
· Keeping up-to-date on pertinent policies, rules and regulations
· Provide legal research as it relates to preceding cases, relevant statues, court decisions, legal articles and other writings
· Assists in preparing drafts of legal documents including lease, real estate, contracts and other items as necessary
· Assists in preparing documents for court cases including legal arguments, court pleadings or motions
· Investigation as needed and deemed necessary as related to the task/case to gather relevant information
· Perform any other function assigned
· Performance is reviewed through reports, general observations, and one-on-one meetings.
MINIMUM REQUIREMENTS
· A.S. or B.S. in administrative or business studies from accredited college or university or a combination of education and experience
· 5 – 7 years minimum experience as a legal secretary or legal administrative assistant
· Indiana Notary Certification strongly preferred
· High level of skill in the proper use of English language (in both verbal and written mode)
· Advanced experience in computer/Microsoft software
· Excellent written and oral communication skills
· Proven analytical skills are a plus
· Ability to listen and communicate; exercising good judgment to form decisions and carry out assigned duties
· A valid driver’s license and demonstrated good driving record
·
