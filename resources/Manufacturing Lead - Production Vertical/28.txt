Duties/Responsibilities
· Manage a team responsible for the scale up of new products and supervise implementation of new and improved manufacturing processes.
· Establish a clear understanding of the capability of existing manufacturing assets and provide guidance for improving asset capability and utilization.
· Ensure operational excellence via efficient manufacturing processes. Set and work towards goals for continuous improvement.
· Assist with supervisory responsibilities for department engineers including performance management evaluations, career development support and balancing engineer’s workload against project deadlines.
· Meet with team regularly to review project status and issue department monthly reports.
· Confer with management, production, business representatives and engineers to discuss project status.
· Coordinate, prioritize and execute projects by making detailed plans to accomplish goals. Direct the integration of technical activities.
· Lead multiple plant support projects both domestically and internationally.
· Assist with the scale up of new products from the synthesis lab thru the pilot plant and into manufacturing.
· Lead and coordinate raw material qualification projects by orchestrating a diverse and international team of engineers, scientists and business personnel.
· Monitor project progress against budgets and milestones using the Lubrizol Stage Gate system for project management.
· Write manufacturing authorization proposals to document raw material changes, new product introductions or process improvements.
· Organize and lead monthly and quarterly cross-functional global plant support process technology meetings. Drive for results by documenting and monitoring progress against objectives.
· Provide equipment scoping assistance as needed.
· Lead the transfer of technology from one manufacturing site to another or externally through tollers.
Skills, Qualifications, Experience
· Validated savings from process improvements ideas with a track record for driving projects to completion.
· Bachelor’s degree in chemical engineering from an accredited university.
· Minimum 8-10 years process engineering experience in a manufacturing environment.
· Previous supervisory experience of a team of engineers considered a plus.
· Six Sigma or Lean manufacturing experience with a specific focus on productivity improvements.
· Excellent organizational, interpersonal and communication skills. Capable of giving persuasive presentations to upper management and communicating with international colleagues.
· Basic knowledge of polymerization chemistry and emulsion polymerization experience highly desirable.
